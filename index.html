ABOUT US

“व्यक्ति बड़ा और महान बाहृााडम्बर से, पहनावे-ओढ़ावे से नहीं, वरन् कर्तव्य से बनता है, चिन्तन-चऱि़त्र से बनता है।”

Quality is never an accident; it is always the result of intelligent effort. This is what we strive to realize through C.F. Andrews International School. We believe that, like everything else, education needs to be in a ‘progressive mode’, especially in this fast-changing world. As we see, the number of schools is rapidly on the rise with fast expansion of education. However, the question is: are all these schools worldwide and especially in India, ready to embrace the change which has become inevitable in today’s progressive, global society? What elements are needed to be imbibed in delivery of education so that it meets the needs of time, is compatible with international standards and is able to review and evaluate its outcome in order to improve? In other words, how many of them are truly ‘Leaders’ in the arena of children education?

C.F. Andrews International School is the Indian offshoot of this global organization.

We are an elite organization striving to add a new dimension to schools worldwide by creating a unique progressive environment for the new age global kids. As we all are aware, we live in a rapidly changing time where only a progressive outlook can ensure our survival and growth. This calls for changing the mind sets of our school leaders and bind them in a common vision as well as unity of purpose so that a fostering environment is created for optimizing the inherent potential, aptitude and creative instinct of every child.

At ‘C.F. Andrews International School, we are dedicated to help the member schools to excel and create new benchmarks in the field of children’s education by continuously raising their own standards. We believe that in this age of rat-race, quality schools have to make a niche for themselves by adopting best practices, best technology, inclusiveness, care for environment, contribution to the human society and smart pedagogy to cater to the developmental needs of every child.

With such a holistic mission and far-sighted strategies, we continue to increase our membership in India and are eager to collaborate with them. We believe that the member schools will learn from the experiences of one another and ‘grow together’ leveraging from this rich milieu of insights gained from schools creating a benchmark in different settings and nurturing children from varied backgrouds.

To reiterate, the ‘C.F. Andrews International School intends to help its member schools excel and create new benchmarks in the field of education by continuously raising their own standards.

Undoubtedly, this goal is very ‘intangible’ and cannot be immediately measured in terms of ‘material deliverables’. However, in course of time its visible results will manifest themselves in many countable terms. Moreover, every organization keeps evolving along its experience with increasing participation of its members and continuous consultation with them. As such, the purpose of and the services rendered by CFAIS will keep crystallizing with the passage of time. At this juncture, we do not intend to involve into tall talks.

Moreover, CFAIS gives the quality schools of India a common, globally-connected platform for learning and sharing as well as for bringing their noble efforts into limelight, because if the world does not know you as a ‘Quality’ school, all your efforts will be like winking in the dark.

‘C.F. Andrews International School is an accrediting body but it is far different from other accrediting agencies which often give out a ranking based on material gains. Accordingly, their so-called ‘Ranking’ keeps changing year by year.

We want to bring transparency, sense of justice and an approach of genuine appreciation in this field.

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

The Major object of C.F. Andrews International School is to educate the youth of the nation in a spirit of service to humanity'. The institution’s priorities in education are not only scholastic excellence but also formation of youth in discipline, hard work. Moral and religious values.

These priorities are meant to prepare youth for life, by promoting intellectual excellence, uprightness of character, emotional maturity, and scientific temper. spirit of healthy competition, co-operation and sportsmanship through co-curricular activities, secular outlook, religious tolerance and moral sensitivity to the needs and rights of others.

The institution is open to all, irrespective of caste, community or religion and strives to fulfill the needs and aspiration of all.

The institution ensures that the students acquire an aesthetic view of life going beyond materialism. The institution pledges therefore to impart to the students a sense of their own dignity and a respect for the dignity and rights of others.

GENERAL RULE OF SCHOOL

  • The School does not hold any responsibility for Transport of students commuting by their own fleet/govt. buses/private operators

  • The school authorities will not be responsible for sudden illness, injury or some natural calamities during the school hours. In case of any accident of the school bus or any other conveyance provided by the school, the management takes no liability what so ever of any damage otherwise.

  • Student should be punctual and regular 80% attendance is compulsory for promotion to the next class. A pupil who has been suffering from or who has been in contact with any infectious disease must produce a certificate (from a competent Doctor) that he may attend School without risk of conveying Infection.

  • Students should be habitually clean and always neatly dressed. The school uniform should be worn on all working days and for all school functions. Students who are not in proper uniform will not be allowed to enter their class rooms and parents will be called to the school to take their wards backhome.

  • 2 sets of Regular Summer Uniform and 1 set of PT uniform will have to be purchase initially .

  • The Tie or Belt are integral parts of the uniform and should always be worn.

  • Only Bata-Naughty Boy/Scout or Action School Time style shoes should be worn by boys. Girls should come in Black leather (Bata Ballerina Style) shoes . Only white canvas shoes should be worn for PT. Sport shoes are not allowed.

  • Girls should keep their hair neatly plaited (two plaits).

  • Boys should have their hair neatly cut and beards trimmed.

  • Nails should be kept short Applying Nail polish, Tattoo or Mehandi is strictly prohibited.

  • Wearing of ornaments is strictly prohibited.

  • Identity Card issued by the school will have to be displayed prominently.

  • No one will be allowed to meet the student doing or after school hours. If friends or acquaintances of any student are found loitering outside the campus, the said student will be given a TC.

  • Mobile Phones and Electronic devices like Radios, MP3/MP4 Players, Calculators, Digital Cameras etc. are strictly banned. If found they will be confiscated by the school.

  • Students should not wear flashy wrist watches. Only those in Class VI and above can wear wrist watches.

  • Irregular attendance, habitual idleness, neglect of home work will justify disciplinary action.

  • Loitering about on the campus without any reason or entering the Library/Computer Lab/ Accounts Office without permission or in the absence of the School Staff will lead to serious disciplinary action.

  • If anyone is found chewing Tobacco, Pan Masala, Smoking or taking any narcotic substance while in school uniform, (within and outside the campus) he/she will be rusticated and T.C. will be issued.

  • Any damage to the school property will be dealt with strictly and will incurr a monetary fine apart from disciplinary action.

  • Disobedience and misbehavior towards staff members or repeated breaking of school rules or behavior objectionable to the good tone of the school, even outside the school premises will deserve severe punishment or even dismissal, if warnings are ignored.

  • Immorality, bad conduct, grave insubordination, contempt of authorities or willful damage to property is always a sufficient reason for immediate dismissal.

  • Any change in address or telephone number should be communicated to the class teacher or at the reception without delay.

  • Every student must bring School Diary to school daily for taking down daily homework and other instructions from the teachers.

CODE OF CONDUCT FOR STUDENTS

  1. They should take good care of their health and keep themselves robust and strong.

  2. They should always be friendly to others in and out of school.

  3. They should avoid vulgarity in talk and behaviors. All students should converse in English both within and outside the school campus.

  4. They should accept whatever work is assigned to them as their rightful share.

  5. They should face difficulties courageously.

  6. They should always be ready to lend a helping hand at home to their parents, brothers and sisters, in school to teachers, companions and any unattended visitor whom they happen to meet in the school premises.

  7. They should never cheat or be unfair at play.

  8. They should rise and properly wish any teacher or visitor who comes to talk to them.

  9. They should be truthful.

  10. They should never be cruel and should know that cruelty is the trait of a bully while kindness is a mark of a gentleman.

  11. They should respect the beauty of the class room and of the school premises and report any damage they may observe to the teachers.

  12. They should never hesitate to say 'No' when asked or tempted to do a thing which they know to be wrong.

  13. They should respect the liberty and the rights of others.

  14. Instead of lamenting and grumbling over the evils of the world they should try to contribute their share in making it a better place to live in.

  15. They should look upon people from all parts of the world and all walks of life as their brethren, irrespective of their caste, colour and creed.

  16. They should take pride in wearing their school uniform and strive to keep it neat and tidy at all times.

ABSENCE / LATE ARRIVAL IN SCHOOL

  1. Parents are requested not to enter classrooms to meet their children or teachers during school hours.

  2. Parents should inform the school if there is any change in their address and telephone numbers in the fixed Performa available at school or Administrative Office.

  3. Leave for half day should not be asked for (for security reasons).In emergency, permission must be sought in writing from the Co-ordinator through the Class Teacher.

  4. Children when sick should not be sent to school to attend classes. If they fall sick on the day of weekly test they may be sent to the school. However after finishing the test if the student seeks permission to return home, the permission will be granted only if the school’s Para medical staff decides that the student should go back.

  5. Parents attention is drawn to the fact that criticism of the school or teacher in the presence of a child should be avoided because it causes the students to lose respect for their teachers with the consequent failure to learn from them thus retarding their progress.

  6. Should there be any legitimate complaint, please meet the Principal / Co-ordinator at the earliest or write to him/her so that the complaint can be personally looked into.

  7. An amount of Rs. 100 will be charged for replacement of Almanac and Rs.50 for I-Card if the same are lost or damaged.

  8. Students are advised not to bring valuable articles for e.g. expensive watches, fountain pens, transistors, cameras, calculators, mobile, I-Pad, Phones or Jewellery to school. In case of loss of valuables or other articles, the authorities will try their best to trace the items out but shall not undertake any responsibility for the loss.

  9. All students are expected to join the school on the notified date at the beginning of the semester or after each vacation.

  10. Parents are requested to personally see off and receive their wards at bus stops along with their identity card (Parent’s Copy).

  11. Collection and distribution of lunch packets and other belongings is not the responsibility of the school.

  12. Parents can e-mail their grievances/suggestions to the Principal and expect prompt reply from the school E-mail.

  13. The name of student will be struck off the rolls if he/she is continuously absent for three days without any written information.

  14. If a child remains absent on the day of exam due to medical reasons, information regarding the same should be given to the school on the same day. No request for retest will be entertained later.

  15. Heavy pocket money should not be given to the students as it leads to the habit of overspending.

  16. No Outside vehicles are allowed in school premises and in case of theft, school bears no responsibility.

ABSENCE / LATE ARRIVAL IN SCHOOL

  1. No student who has been absent on the previous day will be admitted to the class until the parent fills up the 'Record for Non - Attendance' for each day the student is absent from school starting the reasons for absence. Failing this the concerned parent will be called to school to take his/her ward back home.

  2. A student, returning to school after suffering from an infectious or contagious disease, should produce a doctor's fitness certificate permitting him/her to do so.

  3. Repeated absence without leave or unexplained absence for more than seven consecutive days renders the student liable to have his/her name struck off the rolls. Re-admission may be granted only after securing permission of the principal and payment of a fresh admission fee.

  4. All students are expected to attend school on the opening day after the vacations. Absence for more than three days immediately after a vacation would attract strict disciplinary action, After six days, the name of the student will be struck off the rolls.

  5. It is compulsory for the students to complete 75% of attendance in each semester to make them eligible to take the semester examination. The percentage can be relaxed on genuine medical grounds.

  6. Student should be punctual. Late comers will be fined Rs.20/- per day if allowed to attend class.

  7. Student should be in proper Uniform for improper uniform students will be fined Rs.20/- per day.

WITHDRAWALS

  1. One clear calendar month's notice in writing or a month's fee in lieu of notice be given before the pupil is withdrawn.

  2. Those who leave school in between session must in all cases pay the installment of the quarter.

  3. Transfer Certificate is not issued until all the dues of the school are settled.

  4. T.C. will be issued after 5 days from the date of receipt of application.
